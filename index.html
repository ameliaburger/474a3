This visualization explores how the graduation rates of low income high school seniors in Seattle and South King County are influenced by the percentage of graduating seniors who took rigorous courses such AP or IB courses. A student is determined to be low income if they are on Free/Reduced Price Lunch (FRPL).

Size the boxes of the treemap based on the percentage of the school's population that is low income or the total number of students in the school to examine how these values may influence graduation rate. Filters for the percentage of Black/African American students in each school and the percentage of Hispanic/Latino students in each school are also available to examine racial impact.
